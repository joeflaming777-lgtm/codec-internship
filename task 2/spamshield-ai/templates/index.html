💡 Security Tips

Stay Safe Online

🔗
Verify links before clicking — Hover over links to see the real URL. Legitimate companies don't use random domains or URL shorteners in security emails.
📬
Check the sender's address — Phishing emails often use addresses that look official but have subtle misspellings (e.g., "paypa1.com" instead of "paypal.com").
⏱️
Urgency is a red flag — Legitimate organizations don't create panic. Phrases like "Act now!" or "Expires in 24 hours!" are classic pressure tactics.
🎁
If it sounds too good to be true, it is — You didn't win a lottery you didn't enter. Free prizes, unclaimed inheritances, and miracle cures are scams.
🔒
Never share credentials via email — No bank or legitimate service will ever ask for your password, PIN, or full SSN through email.
🛡️
Use AI-powered tools like SpamShield — When in doubt, paste the email content into SpamShield AI for an instant machine-learning classification.
